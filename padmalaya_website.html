I am a PhD Candidate in Strategy and Entrepreneurship at London Business School, developing research on the organizational antecedents and consequences of stakeholder polarization — with particular attention to organizational narratives, rhetorical history, and temporal perceptions.

My dissertation is supervised by Professor Dónal Crilly. Before joining LBS, I worked as a Research Associate in Strategy and Innovation at the Indian School of Business, and prior to academia I spent six years as a Business Consultant at Deloitte and as a Systems Engineer at Infosys.

Scholarship

Research

My work examines how organizational narratives and rhetorical history shape stakeholder polarization and how firms navigate the temporal and relational consequences that follow.

01

Stakeholder Polarization

Examining the organizational antecedents and consequences of polarization among stakeholder groups, with attention to how firms respond to increasingly divided audiences.

02

Organizational Narratives

Studying how firms construct, deploy, and revise narratives to manage legitimacy, align stakeholders, and shape interpretations of organizational action over time.

03

Rhetorical History

Investigating how organizations strategically use and reinterpret their own histories as a resource for competitive advantage and stakeholder engagement.

04

Temporal Perceptions

Exploring how actors' orientations toward the past, present, and future influence strategic decision-making and stakeholder relations within organizations.
